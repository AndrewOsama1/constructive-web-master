3 Tips to Be a Successful Real Estate Developer in 2021

As a Real Estate developer, you always have to be planning, investing, and closely monitoring your investment journey in order to make a profit. Here are 3 hints that, we believe, can help you make better profits this year.

# 1. Build Sustainable Partnership

With all the changing regulations and the frequency of changed marketing behavior that took place in 2020/2021, you need to make sure to always surround your company with tenacious partners just like you in almost every field. Starting from consultants, bankers, and even geologists. Not to mention, serve abundantly and with an open heart all of your connections as much as possible. This will make your firm a better social giver by nature and will open the door for you to receive similar services when you're in great need of them.

# 2. Use New Technologies

Technology is on the rise in all markets and Construction isn't an exception!

Poor Management is a killer for your project and you don't need one more obstacle affecting the sky-rocketing of your investments. That's why the use of technologies like digital management tools is crucial to make sure big complex equations and program management are much more easily managed.

  1. For the developer to visualize the project planning and different stages.
  2. For the developer to help their clients visualize the value of the product through a realistic experience of how it feels living inside.

# 3. Risk is not a question of "If" but "When"

This goes along with problem solving. One of the main problems that you have as a real estate developer is that you have to take on a ton of risk in order for it to work. That means that you need to be constantly looking for ways to mitigate that risk. You’ll begin to do this naturally, because as you become more experienced, you’ll realize that the game is all about mitigating the risk that you have to take on.

We've all been through the increasing risks though the past year. A wise mindset will stay sharp and focused to have plan B always when it comes to Forces majeure. Approach risk as an event that will sure take place. So that when it comes, you're ready to act on it and move on. One of the most important important and unseen forms of risk that you should be aware of is "Accountability without authority".

Accountability in Project Management terms, concerns assuring that the project is completed to the satisfaction of the stake holders, on time, and within budget. This means that the PM must assure all the project team members are working cooperatively toward completing the goals of the project and completing their tasks satisfactorily and on time.

Authority means having the ability to make and enforce decisions and to administer consequences and rewards to team members for completing or not completing project tasks.

The Project Manager/Consultant will make day to day decisions without having to escalate to the developer who will consult with a third party causing serious delays. That’s called burning money deliberately and unless that’s your goal, you should set with your consultant their level of authority in the project and communicate it clearly over and over so that they can easily and efficiently act on it.
