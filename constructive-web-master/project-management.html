Project Management

Project management, is the application of knowledge, skills, tools, and techniques to project activities to meet the project requirements.

Project management processes fall into five groups:

  1. Initiating 
  2. Planning 
  3. Executing 
  4. Monitoring and Controlling
  5. Closing 

A project is temporary in that it has a defined beginning and end in time, and therefore defined scope and resources. And a project is unique in that it is not a routine operation, but a specific set of operations designed to accomplish a singular goal. So a project team often includes people who don’t usually work together – sometimes from different organizations and across multiple geographies.

All management is concerned with these, of course. But project management brings a unique focus shaped by the goals, resources and schedule of each project.

Successful projects begin when proper planning & procedures are implemented systematically with a goal coordinate all active coordination, constructability & efficiency. imperative to any project management plan, those controls monitor overall strategy & identify any deviations that may arise. Project controls assess determine actions to prevent or correct issues. Project complexity, organization maturity, team experience, cost, project importance, & other factors determine the level of control needed.
